About

When the first European settlers arrived in what is now Rockbridge County the land was owned by the British government, part of Orange County, but soon to be in the new Augusta County. Initially, land was obtained through patent deeds from the royal governor of Virginia, but in the late 1730s Governor Gooch granted 92,100 acres to Benjamin Borden, Sr., conditioned on his procuring settlers to improve the land.

The Borden grants covered the area north of Woods Creek to near the present Rockbridge/Augusta County line, from just west of Kerrs Creek to the Blue Ridge Mountains. Benjamin Borden, Sr. died in 1743, having signed about 50 deeds, all of them recorded in Orange County. His executors were his widow Zeruiah Borden and his son Benjamin Borden, Jr. Between them, they signed about 120 deeds, all of them recorded in Augusta County.

Benjamin Borden, Jr. died in 1753 and his executors were his widow Magdalene and Archibald Alexander. They executed about 120 deeds that were recorded in Augusta County and beginning in 1778, Rockbridge County. A series of lawsuits among Borden heirs lasted for decades. As a result, several other people executed Borden deeds, including a commissioner named Samuel Clarke, well into the 19th century. Other lawsuits by and against the owners of Borden grants further complicated the process.

Several years ago I assisted some landowners in trying to determine the chain of ownership back to the Borden grants, and found many gaps and errors in the available information, including a popular “Borden map.” Due to vague legal descriptions and other issues, it is nearly impossible to develop a fully accurate map. I wanted to record the essential information about the Borden grants from the deeds that were recorded.

This spreadsheet was made from my review of court records for the counties involved, to provide a chronological listing of the recorded deeds, showing the names of grantees, acreages, and whether those grants appear in the Borden maps.

-Larry Spurgeon
